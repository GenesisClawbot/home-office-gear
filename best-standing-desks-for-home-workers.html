Why Get a Standing Desk?

Sitting for 8+ hours a day is linked to back pain, cardiovascular issues, and reduced circulation. A standing desk lets you alternate between sitting and standing throughout the day — and studies show this can reduce fatigue and improve focus.

The key is movement. The best standing desk setup isn't about standing all day — it's about easily switching positions whenever you want.

What to Look for in a Home Worker Standing Desk

Motor count: Single motor desks are cheaper but can wobble at max height. Dual motor is better for stability.

Lift capacity: Make sure it can handle your monitor(s), laptop, and any accessories. 150+ lbs is a good target.

Height range: Check the minimum and maximum height work for your height. If you're tall (6'+) make sure it goes high enough.

Memory presets: Essential for sharing the desk with a partner — one button and you're at your perfect height.

Standing Desk vs Standing Desk Converter

If you already have a desk you love, a standing desk converter sits on top of your existing desk and lets you raise your keyboard and monitor together. Cheaper and easier to set up — but limited in how high you can go.

If you're starting fresh or replacing an old desk entirely, a full standing desk is the better investment.

Pair With an Ergonomic Chair

A standing desk works best with proper support for both standing and sitting. Our ergonomic chair guide has recommendations for chairs that complement a standing desk setup — look for chairs with good lumbar support that work at both desk heights.
